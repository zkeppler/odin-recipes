Lomo Saltado

Description

Lomo Saltado is a Peruvian dish consisting of tender steak and vegetables in a savory sauce served with rice and french fries. Peru has a long and rich tradition of chinese cooking, owing to the prevalence of Chinese immigrants in the country dating back to the late 19th century. Lomo wears its chinese influence on its sleeve, using the Chinese cooking technique of stir frying, which is implied in the name "saltado".

I learned this recipe from my father in law, who grew up in Peru and came to the United States in the late 1970s.

Ingredients

Lomo Saltado

  • 1.5 lbs tender steak, such as skirt steak, sirloin, or tenderloin
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 yellow bell pepper
  • 3 roma tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp aji amarillo paste (Adjust to your preferred spice level. I like more spice, my wife likes less)
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cilantro (for garnish)

Cilantro Rice

  • 1 cup dry long grain white rice
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup lime juice, or to taste

Fench Fries

  • 2 russett potatoes
  • 1 liter peanut oil
